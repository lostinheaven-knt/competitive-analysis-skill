# Report Audience Lint

Use this reference when drafting, reviewing, or cleaning any reader-facing report generated by this skill.

Its job is simple:
> Make sure the final report is written for the report audience, not for the analyst, skill maintainer, or workflow designer.

This lint applies to:
- formal competitor reports
- GEO reports
- boss summaries that may be circulated
- qualitative shortlist outputs that may be reused in presentations or decision meetings

It does **not** need to be applied to internal engineering notes, implementation plans, skill design memos, or methodology-only documents unless the user asks to turn those into client-facing material.

---

## 1. Core rule
A reader-facing report should answer:
- What is happening?
- Why does it matter?
- What should the reader do next?
- What limits or uncertainty matter to the reader's decision?

A reader-facing report should **not** answer:
- How the analyst will improve the workflow later
- How the skill should be updated
- What maturity stage the draft is at inside an internal production process
- What case-to-skill abstraction or method-generalization work is happening behind the scenes

---

## 2. Allowed content in reader-facing reports
These are generally safe to include when relevant:
- business conclusions
- competitor framing
- evidence-based findings
- decision-oriented recommendations
- business-relevant research gaps
- confidence and limitation notes that affect how the reader should interpret the result
- follow-up research directions framed in business language

Examples:
- "Current evidence is sufficient for directional strategy discussion but not for precise market share estimation."
- "Additional platform research would improve confidence on pricing and hot-SKU competition."
- "The brand should prepare clearer comparison pages and trust signals."

---

## 3. Disallowed content in reader-facing reports
Unless the user explicitly asks for methodology or internal QA commentary, do not include the following in reader-facing reports.

### 3.1 Internal process language
Examples:
- 下一轮
- 下一版
- 再跑一轮
- 工作底稿
- 试跑
- 内部 workflow
- internal workflow
- draft maturity

### 3.2 Skill-maintenance language
Examples:
- 写回 skill
- skill 规范
- skill 更新
- sample-to-skill abstraction
- skill maintainer note
- prompt design note

### 3.3 Maturity labels or production-stage labels
Examples:
- V1
- V2
- V3+
- V4
- MVP
- beta draft
- final draft for internal iteration

### 3.4 Analyst-facing instructions
Examples:
- 建议分析师后续手工验证
- 用某工具再补一轮
- 下次抓平台时先这样做
- what to test manually next inside the workflow

### 3.5 Meta commentary about the writing process
Examples:
- this sample will later be generalized
- this report is mainly for improving the framework
- this is useful for refining the method

---

## 4. Rewrite rules
When you detect internal/process language, rewrite it into reader-facing language.

### 4.1 Rewrite pattern: internal process -> business follow-up
Instead of:
- "下一轮深挖要补平台层"

Use:
- "后续研究建议优先补充平台层证据"
- "若用于经营决策，建议优先补充平台层研究"

### 4.2 Rewrite pattern: draft maturity -> reader confidence
Instead of:
- "这是 V3+ 判断"

Use:
- "当前证据足以支持方向性判断"
- "当前判断适合用于策略讨论，不适合用于精确份额结论"

### 4.3 Rewrite pattern: skill maintenance -> business recommendation
Instead of:
- "写回 skill 时只保留通用规则"

Use:
- remove entirely from the report, or if needed for the reader:
- "建议将该分析框架标准化为后续研究模板"

### 4.4 Rewrite pattern: analyst instruction -> research recommendation
Instead of:
- "建议分析师用 agent-browser 再补证"

Use:
- "建议补充品牌官网、平台页面与用户评价证据"

---

## 5. Quick lint checklist
Before finalizing a reader-facing report, check these questions:

1. Does every recommendation sound like something the report reader can act on or interpret?
2. Are any sections secretly about improving our process rather than helping the reader decide?
3. Are there version labels, internal iteration labels, or workflow-stage labels in headings or body text?
4. Are research gaps written in business language rather than analyst-task language?
5. Would an external reader be confused by any mention of tools, skill updates, or internal iteration logic?

If any answer is yes, rewrite before delivering.

---

## 6. High-risk zones to lint carefully
Pay special attention to these sections, because internal-process leakage often appears there:
- scope / evidence status notes
- limitations
- next steps
- GEO test recommendations
- follow-up research sections
- appendix-style notes
- headings containing version markers or sample labels

---

## 7. Heading hygiene
Headings in reader-facing reports should avoid:
- V1/V2/V3+/V4
- 试跑版 / 样例版 / 底稿 / draft for workflow
- 写回 skill / 方法抽象 / 污染检查

Prefer headings like:
- Executive Summary
- Key Findings
- Recommended Actions
- Research Limits
- Suggested Follow-up Research
- GEO Work Priorities

---

## 8. Special rule for GEO reports
GEO reports often tempt the analyst to include prompt-design or testing-process chatter.
Do not let GEO reports drift into an internal QA memo.

Keep GEO reports focused on:
- which topics matter
- which prompts are business-relevant to test
- where the brand is likely to be recommended or displaced
- what content, proof, and comparison assets should be built

Do not include:
- how the GEO method will later be abstracted into the skill
- internal notes about prompt-set construction for framework maintenance
- sample-to-method generalization commentary

---

## 9. Default decision rule
If a sentence helps the analyst more than it helps the reader, it probably does not belong in the final report.
