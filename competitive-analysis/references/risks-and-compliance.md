# Risks and Compliance

Use this file when the task could produce overconfident claims, public-facing comparisons, or evidence built on weak signals.

## Hard boundaries
- Do not invent market share, revenue, user count, or pricing facts.
- Do not present guesses as verified facts.
- Do not imply access to confidential or non-public competitor information.
- Do not recommend illegal data collection.

## Common risk areas

### Weak evidence dressed up as certainty
This happens when buzz, traffic, or a few reviews are treated as proof of market position.

### Mismatched comparisons
This happens when candidates come from different geographies, customer segments, or time periods.

### Public-facing comparison risk
If the result might be used in sales, marketing, or advertising, avoid absolute or inflammatory language. Encourage legal or business review before external publication.

### Privacy and data collection risk
When using user feedback, reviews, or scraped public data, avoid collecting unnecessary personal data and stay within platform rules and local law.

## Safe wording
Prefer phrasing like:
- based on available public information
- appears to compete in
- likely belongs in the candidate set
- evidence is limited or mixed
- this ranking is scope-dependent

Avoid phrasing like:
- definitely the market leader, unless strongly supported
- clearly inferior, unless the evidence is very strong and the context requires it
- proven, unless the claim is truly proven

## Final caution
A competitor shortlist is always conditional on the defined scope, evidence quality, and time period. Say that plainly.
