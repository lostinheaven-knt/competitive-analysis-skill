# Source Priority

Use this file when deciding what evidence is strong enough to support inclusion, ranking, or claims.

## Evidence hierarchy

### Tier 1: primary or official
Use first when available.
- Company website and product pages
- Annual reports, filings, prospectuses, investor presentations
- Regulatory disclosures
- Official pricing pages and documentation
- Industry association or government statistics

Best for:
- Product scope
- Pricing model
- Geographic presence
- Official customer cases
- Declared market positioning

Risk:
- May be selective or promotional

### Tier 2: strong secondary
Use to complement or validate Tier 1.
- Reputable industry reports
- Well-known research firms
- Reliable market intelligence platforms
- High-quality analyst writeups

Best for:
- Market share estimates
- Traffic trends
- category rankings
- market landscape context

Risk:
- Methodology may differ across providers

### Tier 3: supporting or exploratory
Use for signals and leads, not as sole support for strong claims.
- News articles
- Media interviews
- Social media metrics
- Community discussions
- App reviews and marketplace comments
- E-commerce reviews and storefront observations

Best for:
- User sentiment
- Recent campaigns or launches
- Brand buzz
- emerging signals

Risk:
- Noisy, biased, and easy to over-read

## How to use sources
- Prefer Tier 1 and Tier 2 for shortlist decisions.
- Use Tier 3 to generate leads, validate relevance, or add color.
- If only Tier 3 exists, weaken the claim and label the evidence as tentative.

## Conflict handling
When sources disagree:
1. Do not hide the disagreement.
2. Prefer the source with clearer scope, date, and methodology.
3. Check whether the numbers refer to different markets, periods, or definitions.
4. If unresolved, keep both views and explain the ambiguity.

## Citation discipline
For meaningful claims, capture:
- source name
- date or access date
- what the source supports
- whether the figure is official, estimated, or inferred

## Claim strength guide
- Strong: backed by Tier 1 or multiple aligned Tier 2 sources
- Moderate: backed by one good Tier 2 source or multiple partial sources
- Weak: backed mainly by Tier 3 or inference

Do not present weak claims as settled facts.
